Before

The Calm Before the Collapse

Earth thrived with giant insects, amphibians, and early reptiles. Lush forests blanketed the land, and shallow seas teemed with life — until volcanic rifts began to poison the skies.

During

The Great Dying

Massive volcanic eruptions in Siberia released CO₂ and methane, causing runaway global warming. Acidic oceans suffocated marine life, while wildfires and toxic air wiped out land species.

After

Life Rebuilds

From the ashes, small, resilient reptiles and synapsids survived. Ferns and fungi dominated the land. Within a few million years, early dinosaurs began to rise in the new world they inherited.
